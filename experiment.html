text tube experiment

initial setup

There are 4 test tubes of each 100ml (x4= 400ml total capacity) 2 | 2 in pairs.
Each pairs have been allocated a volume of 100ml of water (x2= 200ml total water).

  1. In 1st pair, each of the 2 test tubes are filled with 100ml of water equally, 50ml each. Consistency pair
  2. In 2nd pair, one of the test tubes is fill fully with 100ml of water and another is left empty. Uncertainty pair

questions
  1. Which pair is better and more useful than the another? Why?
  2. How to fill the empty portion of the pairs? In the sense how to cope for addition 200ml of water required to fill 400ml capacity of test tube?

experiment explanation

We have 2 sets (pairs) and we are going to run a probability exercise on both of them seperatly. We are going to choose a test tube at random for N number of times. Obervations are below,

  1. Consistency set { 50ml, 50ml } - 2 half filled test tubes
    • It is clearly evident that we will get 50ml always.
    • The probability to get 0ml is 0. We will get water all the time.
    • The probability to get 100ml is also 0. No matter what we do, we will never get more than 50ml
  2. Uncertainty set { 100ml, 0ml } - 1 fully filled test tube, 1 empty test tube
    • There is an uncertainty, we may get full 100ml jackpot or total loss 0ml.
    • The probability to get 0ml is 0.5, we may get nothing in a try.
    • The probability to get 100ml is also 0.5, we may get jackpot also.

Now look at those questions,

  1. Which is better? Consistency or Uncertainty?
  2. How to solve the problems either with consistency set or with uncertainty set?
Our goal is to get fully filled test tube all the time upon choosing randomly with high efficiency.


axioms
  1. Should not intermix the water across the pairs. No operations inbetween the sets should be done. Each set is an isolated distribution.
  2. Should not throw away the empty test tube. In other words should not reduce the elements in the set.*

example solution
  1. In the uncertainty pair, probability of getting 100ml is 0.5.
    We need to find an algorithm which will always pick the 100ml test tube on each draw.
    (i.e) If we can see the test tube before picking, we shall pick the right one always.
    Here 'seeing' is the algorithm.

  2. For the sake of experiment, let us just say have we the power to control the time.
    We can squeeze the progress of time to almost 0 and we can traverse through the time very fast almost ∞.

    Now follow the steps,

    1. Freeze the time.
    2. Pour the water from fully filled test tube to the empty one.
    3. Run the time for the lowest infinitesimal amount as possible.
    4. Continue the above 3 steps till the amount of time you ran is measurable, say 1 minute.
    5. Now isolate that minute and run it fast as possible.

    Like a video that is being played faster, you will now observe that both of the test tubes will be having 100ml of water, due to faster time progression.
    Eventhough, we did not produce more water, we produced an illusion that acts like it is filled.

  3. Yes, there is always the option in which we shall produce more water than the given 100ml for both consistency pair and uncertainty pair. For that, we need to break the 1st Law of Thermodynamics.

We have two pairs, consistency pair and uncertainty pair.
We can try to solve any of these or we can try to break the 1st Law of Thermodynamics.
